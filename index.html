Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

Financial sectors are targets of cyber attacks, malware, viruses, hacking and data theft.

Protecting user's assets should be the primary goal in banking sectors, as technology continues to be implemented in everyday banking systems. With the increase of customers conducting transcations online via banking apps and using digital payments methods such as credit and debit cards, banks should implement adequate systems and standarad frameworks that provide protection and best practises.

Here is brief list of 5 tips for financial sectors to enhance their own financial cybersecurity :

  • Utilize two-factor aunthentication on smartphones and apps- Banks can invest in implementing two-factor aunthentication for customers on their banking apps, for example, when a customer purchases a good/service online, their respective bank should send a code to verify their identity. This adds an extra layer of protection for both bank and customer in ensuring safe banking due to increased mobile phone transactions.
  • Introduce 24/7 network monitoring - Install security software that detects attacks and intrusions through antivirus sofware, firewalls and IDS (Intrusion Detection Systems).
  • Vulnerability Testing Framework - Invest in AI Based solutions that are designed to indicate threats by imitating hacker methods, for the purpose assess and anaylse any potential breaches and backdoor vulnerabilities that could be taken advantage of.
  • Have an incident report plan - Implement a plan for employees to follow when faced with a breach, that is clear, concise and straighforward. This also includes providing continuos education and up to date knowledge on cybersecurity to staff.
  • Risk management - Address data security issues, evaluate and prioritiz data integrity on internal portals, websites and security of sensitive information.
